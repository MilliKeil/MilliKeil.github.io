milli keil

I seek to connect various subject areas - including art, design, science, digital media, politics, and performing arts - to break free from habitual thought patterns and pave the way for new structures. My work aims to initiate discourse about multidisciplinary issues that require symbiotic collaboration between design and computation methods.

In October 2022 I started studying the joint masters program Design & Computation between the University of the Arts Berlin and the Technical University Berlin. Previously I studied and worked in the field of economics, with a particular focus on climate policy-making. This led me to complete my bachelor in 'Sustainable Management B.Sc.' at Potsdam Institute for Climate Impact Research (PIK) in the working group for Infrastructure and Complex Networks. Additionally, I work as an extra at the Deutsche Oper Berlin and volunteer at Klasse Klima, a student initiative at UdK, as well as at Stromnetz Berlin GmbH in their Bürger*innenrat. Since 2023, I have been the recipient of a scholarship from the Studienstiftung des Deutschen Volkes (German National Academic Foundation). From September 2024 to February 2025, I studied a guest semester at the Academy of Fine Arts and Design in Bratislava, Slovakia in the Intermedia Studio under Jana Kapelová and Roman Bicek.
